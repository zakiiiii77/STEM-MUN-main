About STEM MUN

Model United Nations (MUN) is an academic simulation where the world's most pressing issues are debated and solved using the same procedures as the UN. At MUN, delegates experience exhilarating parliamentary-style debates on critical global agendas.

STEM Model United Nations (STEM MUN) provides a platform for young diplomats to understand international issues and policies through engaging debates and knowledge sharing. Our participants come from diverse backgrounds, creating an atmosphere where they can enhance diplomatic skills, critical thinking abilities, and build valuable networks.

Through our conferences and training programs, delegates develop essential skills in research, public speaking, negotiation, and leadership - all while gaining a deeper understanding of global diplomacy and international relations.

Our Core Objectives

Knowledge Development

Establish a comprehensive understanding of political science, diplomacy, and critical global issues affecting our world today.

Skill Enhancement

Develop essential skills in research, negotiation, collaboration, leadership, and critical thinking through practical experience.

Global Perspective

Promote a deeper understanding of global issues and cultural diversity while fostering international cooperation and appreciation.

Our Mission

Two fundamental elements serve as the foundation for development initiatives in STEM MUN: one is member-specific, and the other is entity-specific. We strive to help students remold their personalities, broaden their horizons, and improve their social interactions, preparing them for the demands of the professional world.

Our mission includes teaching collaboration and participation in various committees and institutions, while upholding the value of member cooperation. Additionally, we set standards for scientific research to further political objectives, and establish principles for debating viewpoints and ideas in a sophisticated, scientific manner.
